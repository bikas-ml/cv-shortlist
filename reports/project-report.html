Project Report Recruitment Automation System  ·  Confidential
CV Shortlister
AI-Powered Resume Screening & Candidate Evaluation Platform

This document provides a formal, step-by-step overview of the CV Shortlister system — a structured recruitment support platform designed to standardise and accelerate the initial stages of candidate evaluation. The system integrates AI-driven resume analysis, Applicant Tracking System (ATS) keyword matching, and exam-based assessment into a single, traceable workflow. Two roles interact with the system throughout this workflow: the Applicant, who submits credentials and completes assessments when invited, and the HR Professional, who reviews scored outputs, makes shortlisting decisions, administers examinations, and records final hiring outcomes.

System Type Recruitment Support
User Roles Applicant & HR
Evaluation Method AI + ATS + Exam
Scoring Approach Conservative · Evidence-Only
1. Project Overview

CV Shortlister is a two-role recruitment platform in which an Applicant submits a PDF résumé and the system evaluates it automatically against a predefined job description. The HR professional then reviews the scored output, makes shortlisting or rejection decisions, administers an optional examination, and records a final hiring outcome. By standardising the initial screening stage, the system removes manual inconsistency and provides HR with evidence-backed summaries for every candidate.

2. Applicant Workflow — Step-by-Step

The following steps describe the complete journey of an Applicant from initial registration through to final exam submission. Each step is sequential; the next step becomes available only after the current one is successfully completed.

1
→
Account Registration & Authentication
The Applicant begins by creating a new account on the platform. A unique email address and a secure password are required for registration. Upon successful account creation, the system authenticates the Applicant and grants access to the candidate submission portal. This step ensures that every application is tied to a verified and uniquely identifiable individual, which preserves the integrity of the hiring record.
2
→
Résumé Upload in PDF Format
After logging in, the Applicant uploads their curriculum vitae in PDF format. The system accepts only PDF documents to ensure consistent and reliable text extraction across all submissions. The uploaded file is stored securely and immediately queued for automated analysis. No manual reformatting or editing of the document is required from the Applicant at this stage.
3
→
Automated Résumé Analysis Against Job Description
The system extracts the full text from the uploaded PDF and compares it strictly against the active job description. The analysis produces an AI overall score, a skills match percentage, an experience match rating, an education match assessment, an ATS keyword score, a list of identified strengths, a list of competency gaps, and a summary of missing requirements. Only evidence explicitly present in the résumé is considered; no assumptions or inferences are made on the candidate's behalf.
4
→
Awaiting HR Review & Shortlist Decision
Once analysis is complete, the Applicant enters a structured waiting state. The HR professional independently reviews the analysis output alongside the original résumé. At this stage, HR may shortlist the candidate — advancing them to the examination phase — or reject the application. The Applicant is notified of the outcome through the platform, ensuring transparency at every stage of the process.
5
→
Examination Receipt, Completion & Submission
If shortlisted, the Applicant receives an examination consisting of 15 multiple-choice questions tailored to the requirements of the role. The Applicant completes all questions and submits their answers within the allotted time period. The submitted answers are recorded by the system and made immediately available to the HR professional for evaluation alongside the résumé analysis results.
3. HR Professional Workflow — Step-by-Step

The HR professional operates independently from the Applicant and maintains full visibility over all submissions at every stage. The following steps describe the HR journey from candidate pool review through to the recording of a final hiring decision.

1
→
Candidate Pool Review & Dashboard Access
The HR professional logs into the administrative dashboard and is presented with a complete list of all submitted applications. Each entry displays the candidate's name, submission date, and a summary of their primary evaluation scores. HR may filter or sort the pool by score, submission date, or application status to prioritise the review queue. This overview provides an immediate, structured picture of the entire candidate pool.
2
→
Detailed Analysis Inspection
For each candidate under consideration, the HR professional opens the detailed analysis panel. This view presents the AI overall score, the ATS keyword match score, the skills alignment percentage, the experience fit rating, and the education match assessment. Additionally, the system displays explicitly identified strengths, competency gaps, and missing requirements. HR uses this structured output — in conjunction with the original résumé — to form a fully informed and well-supported judgment regarding each applicant.
3
→
Shortlist or Rejection Decision
Based on the analysis results and their professional judgment, the HR professional records a formal decision — Shortlist or Reject — for each candidate. Shortlisted candidates proceed to the examination phase. Rejected candidates are marked accordingly, and their records remain within the system for audit and reference purposes. Every decision is logged with a timestamp, ensuring full traceability and accountability throughout the process.
4
→
Examination Paper Distribution
For candidates who have been formally shortlisted, the HR professional issues the examination paper through the platform. The system delivers the examination directly to the candidate's account and notifies them accordingly. HR retains full control over when the examination is issued and can monitor which candidates have received, commenced, or completed it. This step removes the need for manual coordination and keeps the assessment stage organised within a single system.
5
→
Examination Review & Final Hiring Decision
Once an Applicant submits their examination, the HR professional accesses the completed answers for review. The examination results are presented alongside the original résumé analysis to support a comprehensive and holistic final evaluation. The HR professional then records the definitive hiring decision — Selected or Rejected — within the system. This decision concludes the hiring cycle for that candidate and is stored as a permanent, auditable record within the platform.
4. Key Evaluation Signals

The system computes four primary evaluation signals for each résumé submission. All signals are derived strictly from the textual content of the uploaded résumé measured against the job description. No inferred, assumed, or supplemented information is incorporated into any score.

Signal Source Module Description
AI Overall Score AI Engine A holistic percentage reflecting how well the résumé content aligns with the complete requirements of the job description, combining skills, experience, and education into a single composite measure.
Skills Match AI Engine The proportion of skills explicitly listed in the job description that can be identified and verified within the résumé text. Only skills supported by direct textual evidence are included in this score.
Experience Match AI Engine An assessment of the candidate's years of experience, role titles, and stated responsibilities, measured against the experience requirements defined in the job description. Only verifiable experience is counted.
ATS Keyword Score ATS Module The frequency and contextual relevance of job-description keywords found in the résumé, combined with an evaluation of résumé formatting quality as it would be interpreted by an Applicant Tracking System parser.

▸ Scoring principle: The system applies conservative, evidence-only logic. Any requirement not explicitly verifiable in the résumé text is treated as absent and scored accordingly. This ensures that all evaluation outputs reflect confirmed candidate credentials exclusively.

6. Value Delivered to Each Role
For the Applicant

The submission process is straightforward and requires only a valid account and a PDF résumé. No manual form completion or interpretation of scoring criteria is required from the Applicant. The platform communicates the status of the application at each key stage — analysis completion, shortlist or rejection outcome, and examination receipt — ensuring that the Applicant always has clear visibility of their position within the hiring process.

For the HR Professional

HR receives a structured, evidence-backed summary for every candidate without performing the initial text review manually. Filtering by score or application status significantly reduces the time spent on clearly unsuitable submissions. Every decision — shortlist, rejection, examination distribution, and final outcome — is logged within the system, making the entire process auditable, defensible, and straightforward to communicate to stakeholders.

7. Conclusion
CV Shortlister is a structured recruitment support system that integrates AI-driven résumé analysis, ATS-style keyword matching, and examination-based candidate assessment into a unified two-role hiring workflow. By applying a conservative, evidence-only scoring approach, the system ensures that every evaluation is grounded exclusively in verifiable candidate data rather than assumptions or inferences. The result is a hiring process that is faster and more consistent for HR, transparent and straightforward for Applicants, and fully traceable for organisational records — regardless of the volume of applications received.
